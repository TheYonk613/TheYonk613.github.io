Background

Taking big ideas and making them actually work.

My name is Netanel Younker. Since my first role out of university, I've lived in the space where technology meets operations. Even back in school, my friend group was constantly building servers and writing programs to make their lives run faster. My role in the group? Understanding what they were trying to make happen — and figuring out the structure to actually do it properly.

That dynamic never really stopped. The most common blind spot I see in business is focusing entirely on revenue and vision while ignoring how the day-to-day actually moves. A great idea that takes 40 hours of manual work a week isn't a great business — it's a trap.

I built YounkerStudio because I saw too many small businesses hiring three different vendors — a web person, an automation person, a strategy person — and ending up with a fractured system where nobody owns the full picture. I'm one person. You bring me the problem, and I build the system that solves it.

How I Operate

The 4 rules I don't
compromise on.

Rule 01

Figure it out first

No code is written, no tool is bought, no workflow is automated until we actually understand the root problem. Symptoms and causes are different things.

Rule 02

Simple over clever

Clever systems break. Simple systems scale. I don't build Rube Goldberg machines to make myself look smart — I build things that work when I'm not in the room.

Rule 03

The unvarnished truth

If an idea won't work, or a tool is wrong for the job, I'll say it plainly. You're paying for honest judgment, not for someone to agree with you.

Rule 04

You own it all

Every deliverable, every line of code, every tool account — it belongs to you. I build systems you own outright, not dependencies on my continued involvement.
